About me

Hi, I’m Bryce. I'm a 2nd year Computer Science Master’s student at the University of Southern California. I completed my undergraduate degree in Biology at the University of Washington, then spent 2.5 years in the biotech industry working in spatial biology. There, I helped develop advanced imaging systems to visualize and quantify gene expression. This work sparked my transition into the world of machine learning and computer science.

I’m originally from San Ramon in the Bay Area, and I’m passionate about building intelligent systems that integrate language and vision. My current interests lie in multimodal large language models (LLMs), NLP, and computer vision — especially their applications in areas like autonomous driving. My long-term goal is to become an ML engineer working at the cutting edge of these technologies.

Outside of tech, I’ve hiked Half Dome, ran a marathon, and competed internationally in junior circuit for badminton. Staying active, pushing boundaries, and connecting with new people are all a big part of who I am.
