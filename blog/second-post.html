Year 1 at USC

📈 Growth Over the Past Year

Since coming to USC, it has been an incredible year of growth personally and professionally.

Aspects of school that I appreciate: flexibility in dedicating your full attention into pursuing your passions. So far, I’ve spent the majority of Friday nights and weekends doing projects, assignments, and attending talks/seminars—but I don’t mind. This is a worthwhile sacrifice for the future and something I want to do.

I still remind myself to take breaks and hang out with friends!

💻 Coding Journey

Looking back, I can finally say that I feel comfortable coding and able to identify high-level solutions to coding problems—something I wouldn’t have thought possible a year ago. Side note: I still struggle with bugs in my code.

With GPT, minuscule errors are becoming less relevant, and more emphasis is placed on high-level thinking while AI handles the small stuff. This aligns with what our algorithms class (CSCI 570) teaches: focus on high-level approaches.

🎓 USC’s MSCS Program

I don’t regret coming into this program one bit—in fact, I feel extremely lucky. It's filled with smart people from across the world. The cohort is roughly 80% international and 20% domestic, with many from top universities like IIT, NTU, UCSD, Berkeley, etc.

Some might call it imposter syndrome, but honestly, I do feel behind the average in terms of grades and CS knowledge—I'm coming from an unrelated bachelor’s, so it’s an uphill battle.

Class sizes vary: common classes like NLP, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Algorithms have 300+ students, while niche electives may have just 30.

🛠️ Scientist and Engineer Track

I’m part of the Scientist and Engineers track, which is designed for those with an unrelated bachelor’s. It’s been a perfect fit. I continue to meet a diverse group with similar struggles—majors include Chemical Engineering, Math, Biomed, Aerospace, and more.

I really appreciate this track. Transitioning from biotech to CS felt uncommon and isolating when I was working. Now, surrounded by driven individuals hustling to catch up, I feel inspired every day.

👥 Social Support at USC

Something that caught me by surprise is the level of social support USC provides. I’ve met countless people (undergrad and grad) through university-hosted events, seminars, socials, and talks. I didn’t have this in undergrad, and now it motivates me to make even more connections.
